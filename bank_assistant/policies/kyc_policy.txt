POLICY NAME: Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy
POLICY CODE: KYC-002
LAST UPDATED: January 2024

OVERVIEW:
KYC is a mandatory process to verify the identity of customers to prevent fraud, money laundering,
and other financial crimes. All employees must adhere to this policy strictly.

WHEN KYC IS REQUIRED:
- New account opening
- Large cash transactions (above $10,000)
- Wire transfers above $5,000
- Loan applications
- Changes to customer personal information
- Suspicious activity detected on an account

KYC LEVELS:
1. Basic KYC (Standard Customers)
   - One government-issued photo ID (Passport, Driver's License, National ID)
   - One proof of address (utility bill, bank statement - not older than 3 months)

2. Enhanced KYC (High-Risk or High-Value Customers)
   - Two government-issued IDs
   - Source of funds declaration
   - Enhanced due diligence report
   - Senior management approval required

3. Corporate KYC (Business Accounts)
   - Certificate of incorporation
   - List of directors and shareholders (with IDs)
   - Memorandum and Articles of Association
   - Business address proof
   - Authorization letter for account operator

ACCEPTABLE IDENTITY DOCUMENTS:
- Passport
- National Identity Card
- Driver's License
- Voter Registration Card (with photo)

ACCEPTABLE ADDRESS PROOF:
- Utility bill (electricity, water, gas) - within 3 months
- Bank statement from another institution - within 3 months
- Government-issued mail (tax letters, registration)
- Lease/rental agreement

KYC VERIFICATION PROCESS:
1. Collect original documents from the customer
2. Verify document authenticity using the ID verification system
3. Take clear scans/photographs of all documents
4. Upload to the KYC Management System (KYCMS)
5. Mark verification status in the customer profile
6. Retain physical or digital copies as per record retention policy (7 years minimum)

KYC RENEWAL:
- Standard customers: Every 3 years
- High-risk customers: Every 1 year
- Corporate accounts: Every 2 years

NON-COMPLIANCE:
Failure to complete KYC may result in:
- Account suspension
- Transaction blocking
- Regulatory penalties to the bank
- Disciplinary action for the responsible employee

ESCALATION:
For KYC disputes or high-risk customer cases, escalate to the Compliance Team.
Contact: compliance@bank.internal
